## LO 9.G: Explain the relative significance of the effects of change in the period after 1980 on American national identity.

**Thesis:** The period after 1980 reshaped American national identity along three dimensions: **economic identity** (from industrial middle class to service/gig economy, from shared prosperity to extreme inequality), **racial/ethnic identity** (from white-majority nation to "majority-minority" by 2045), and **political identity** (from bipartisan consensus to polarized partisan tribes), with the most significant effect being **the loss of trust in institutions** (government, media, science, elections) – from 75% trust (1964) to 25% (2023) – which has fundamentally fragmented any shared sense of what it means to be American.

**Body Evidence (Detailed Bullet Points):**

- **Economic identity transformed (KC-9.2.I.C, KC-9.2.I.D):** The "American Dream" (each generation does better than previous) – true 1945-1973; false 1973-present. Millennials: first generation to do worse than parents (median income 20% less than parents at same age). Homeownership rate: 69% (2004), 64% (2020) – lowest since 1965. Student debt: $0 (1980), $1.6 trillion (2020). 1 in 5 adults over 40 has no retirement savings.
- **Middle class shrinking (KC-9.2.I.D):** Share of adults in middle class (income 67%-200% of median): 61% (1970), 52% (1980), 50% (2020). Lower class: 25% (1970), 29% (2020). Upper class: 14% (1970), 21% (2020). "We are the 99%" (Occupy Wall Street 2011). "Socialism" approval: 40% (2020) among under-30s – highest since 1940s.
- **Racial/ethnic identity transformed (KC-9.2.II.A, KC-9.2.II.B):** White population share: 80% (1980), 60% (2020), projected 49% (2045). No. George Floyd protests (May-June 2020) – 25 million participants; $2 billion damage; curfews in 40 cities. Black Lives Matter founded (2013) – 20 million supporters (2020). - **Political identity polarized (KC-9.2.II.C):** Political polarization measured by vote overlap: 60% overlap (1980), 5% overlap (2020).
